# Continuity Apps

A continuity app is a piece of software built around the question:

> **What keeps showing up through you?**

Most software begins with identity: _Who are you?_  
Continuity apps begin with activity: _What do you keep doing, returning to, resisting, protecting, repeating, revealing?_

The core primitive is:

```text
activity → continuity → identity
```

Identity is not the starting point. Identity is what becomes visible after a person has acted enough times for a pattern to appear.

---

## The simple definition

**Continuity apps are apps where the main thing that compounds is the human, not the platform.**

They are not built primarily to capture attention, grow a feed, optimize engagement, or accumulate data inside a company-owned profile. They are built to help a person stay in relationship with a practice, a rhythm, a trace, a vow, a body, a ritual, or a recurring question.

They preserve continuity without turning the user into a product.

They ask software to become a witness.

---

## The internet frame

One way to frame the history of the internet:

```text
Web1: Read
Web2: Write
Web3: Own
Web4: Continue
```

Continuity is not just logging in with the same account across many apps. It is not simply a portable profile.

Continuity means the same subject can carry memory, permissions, history, rhythm, intent, and authorship across contexts without surrendering themselves to a platform.

A continuity layer is not only about owning assets. It is about preserving the thread of the person.

---

## What continuity apps are not

### Not identity apps

An identity app asks:

> Who are you?

A continuity app asks:

> What keeps showing up through you?

Identity is useful, but it is not the soul of the app. Identity is infrastructure. Continuity is the lived thread.

### Not productivity apps

Productivity software often asks:

> How can you do more?

Continuity software asks:

> What are you becoming through what you keep doing?

It is less concerned with output and more concerned with contact.

### Not social apps

Social apps usually organize around audience, performance, metrics, feeds, reactions, and public signaling.

Continuity apps may touch public signal, but they do not worship it. If they read public traces, they do it to mirror something back with care, not to trap the person inside another feed.

### Not AI companion apps

A continuity app can use AI, but the AI is not the center.

The AI is a mirror, not a teacher.  
A witness, not a guru.  
A reflection surface, not a replacement for the person’s own knowing.

The app should not create dependency on the model. It should return the person to themselves.

---

## The core pattern

A continuity app usually has four movements.

### 1. The user acts

The app begins with an action, not a profile.

The action can be writing, fasting, walking, meditating, making, checking in, training, building, publishing, or any repeated human gesture.

The action is small enough to repeat, but meaningful enough to reveal something.

### 2. The app protects the action

The app creates a boundary around the practice.

It reduces noise. It removes unnecessary choices. It does not ask the user to perform. It does not turn the moment into content too quickly.

The app serves the ritual.

### 3. The action leaves a trace

The app preserves the trace of what happened.

The trace is not just data. It is evidence of contact.

It may include timing, rhythm, silence, completion, recurrence, friction, interruption, return, or refusal.

The trace says: _you were here._

### 4. The trace becomes continuity

Over time, the traces form a thread.

The person begins to see what keeps returning. The app does not need to define the person from above. It can simply reveal the pattern that their own actions have already written.

Continuity is accumulated awareness.

---

## Ownership and memory

A continuity app does not need to store everything on its own servers.

In fact, the cleanest version often does the opposite.

The server is not memory.  
The company is not memory.  
The AI is not memory.

The continuity remains with the practitioner.

This can be expressed through local-first storage, user-owned files, cryptographic signatures, portable artifacts, or a user-held memory profile. The technical implementation can vary, but the principle stays the same:

> The system may witness, but it should not possess.

A continuity app can know enough to serve the next moment without claiming ownership over the whole person.

---

## Anky as a continuity app

Anky is a clear example.

Anky is not a journaling app first.  
It is not an AI companion first.  
It is not a productivity tool first.  
It is not a crypto app first.

Anky is a protected ritual for eight minutes of uninterrupted presence.

The user opens the app, writes, stays, stops, sees what was written, and can optionally ask Anky to mirror it back.

The canonical artifact is the `.anky` file. That file is not only the text. It carries rhythm, timing, silence, and characters. It is the session. It is a trace of presence.

The reflection is not the product’s soul. The writing is.

The account is not the beginning. The writing is the beginning.

Anky can use cryptographic identity underneath. It can use signatures, entitlements, ledgers, or local memory. But those are infrastructure. They should not replace the ritual.

The continuity is not that Anky remembers everything about the user on a server. The continuity is that the person keeps returning to the act, and the act keeps showing them something true.

## The design law

A continuity app should obey a few laws.

### 1. Begin with practice, not profile

Do not make the user explain themselves before they act.

Let the first meaningful thing be the ritual.

### 2. Make the artifact portable

The user should be able to carry the trace with them.

A file, signature, local database, wallet, private backup, or export can become a continuity object. The point is that the person is not trapped inside the platform.

### 3. Let identity emerge

Do not overdefine the user.

Let repeated action reveal pattern. Let the person recognize themselves through what keeps returning.

### 4. Use AI as reflection, not authority

The model should not tell the person who they are.

It should notice, mirror, soften, illuminate, and return agency.

### 5. Compound the human

The most important accumulated value should live in the person: awareness, discernment, self-trust, rhythm, agency, honesty, courage, presence.

If the platform compounds but the human fragments, the app has failed.

### 6. Keep the surface simple

Continuity does not require a complicated “continuity system” on day one.

A local-first app with a protected ritual can already be a continuity app. The philosophy matters more than premature architecture.

---

## Why this matters now

The dominant internet fragments people.

It creates many accounts, many feeds, many masks, many metrics, many little performances of self. The person is spread across platforms, each one extracting a different part of them.

Continuity apps respond to that fragmentation.

They do not ask: _How do we capture more attention?_

They ask:

> How do we help the human stay continuous inside a fragmented internet?

They are software in service of the human.

They return agency.

They make technology less like a casino and more like a witness.

---

## A working sentence

**Continuity apps are cultivation software: apps that protect a repeated human action, preserve its trace, and help the person recognize what keeps showing up through them over time.**

Or shorter:

**Continuity apps are software where the human compounds.**
